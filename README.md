
# Data Analysis Project I – Employee Salary Analysis

## Overview

This project analyzes employee salary data to examine differences across gender and employment categories. The objective is to identify patterns, disparities, and relationships affecting salary distribution within an organization.

---

## Dataset Information

* **Total Records:** 474 employees
* **Variables:**

  * Gender (categorical)
  * Employment Category (categorical)
  * Current Salary (numerical)

### Gender Distribution

* Female: 216 (45.6%)
* Male: 258 (54.4%)

---

## Key Analysis

### 1. Salary Analysis by Gender

| Metric             | Female     | Male       |
| ------------------ | ---------- | ---------- |
| Mean Salary        | $26,031.92 | $41,441.78 |
| Median             | $24,300    | $32,850    |
| Standard Deviation | $7,558     | $19,499    |
| Minimum            | $15,750    | $19,650    |
| Maximum            | $58,125    | $135,000   |

**Insights:**

* Males earn approximately $15,000 more on average.
* Male salaries show significantly higher variability.
* High-paying positions are more commonly held by males.

---

### 2. Gender vs Employment Category

**Female Distribution:**

* Clerical: 95.4%
* Managerial: 4.6%
* Custodial: 0%

**Male Distribution:**

* Clerical: 60.9%
* Managerial: 28.7%
* Custodial: 10.5%

**Insights:**

* Female employees are highly concentrated in clerical roles.
* Male employees are more evenly distributed, especially in managerial positions.

---

### 3. Salary by Employment Category

| Category   | Mean Salary | Median  | Range            | Standard Deviation |
| ---------- | ----------- | ------- | ---------------- | ------------------ |
| Clerical   | $27,838.54  | $26,550 | $15,750–$80,000  | $7,568             |
| Custodial  | $30,938.89  | $30,750 | $24,300–$35,250  | $2,114             |
| Managerial | $63,977.80  | $60,500 | $34,410–$135,000 | $18,244            |

**Insights:**

* Managerial roles have the highest salaries and the greatest variability.
* Clerical roles dominate the workforce but offer lower average salaries.
* Custodial roles have relatively stable and consistent salaries.

---

## Overall Findings

* There is a clear gender pay gap in the dataset.
* Employment category has a strong influence on salary.
* Gender distribution across job categories contributes to salary differences.
* Managerial roles offer the highest compensation and are predominantly occupied by males.

---

## Conclusion

* Clerical positions represent the largest share of employees but provide lower salaries.
* Custodial positions offer consistent but limited salary ranges.
* Managerial positions provide the highest salaries with wide variability.
* Gender imbalance across employment categories is a key factor in salary inequality.
